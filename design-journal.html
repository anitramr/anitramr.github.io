Week Two

Prompt: What are your initial thoughts on flex box vs grid? Are you drawn to one more than the other? What are you planning to do for project 1? What content do you plan to show? How will you make each page unique while maintaining a cohesive design across the project? How will your color palette, imagery, and typography choice complement your subject matter?

Response: For project one I am planning on doing a special on abandond places in Oregon. Some will have a haunted history attached to them and others won't, but all of them are ones that you can go and view - at your own risk for one or two. I'm planning on showing images of the locations/structures and then a short synopsis on the history and lore surrounding the locations/structures. To maintain consistency I plan on using the same typefaces, similar type hierarchy, similar if not the same color palette, and the same filter/appearance of my photos. I aim to make the pages of my website flow smoothly from one to the next. I want to be able to lead the viewers eye in a predictable manner. But each page will be unique in picture placement, text placement, and if I can get it to work, gifs. For my website I want it to have an underlying spooky/eerie theme, but still have that classic modern feel that Oregon tries to portray. To implement this in my type I want to use a classic sans-serif, but one that tends to have taller slim characters to be a bit spookier. When it comes to my imagery I'm leaning into making them ghostly and adding noise into the photos with photoshop. Color palette is where I am still feeling uncertain. I'm thinking a light off-white, mossy green, and then maybe a burnt red or orange? I still have to play with it because I don't want to lose the impression that this is Oregon specific nor do I want to make this website seem like it is strictly Halloween related. This is something I am going to have to play with, but I think I would like to include a pale yellow and possibly a deep purple.

I want this website to be easy to navigate and understand.

Week Eight

Prompt: Read this article on mobile first design and share your thoughts on mobile first vs desktop first.

Response: Personally I find it very challenging to design for mobile first, but I believe I know why. I've always been better at solving problems, such as multiple choice on tests, by first ruling out which answers are not correct. My brain functions better taking away things rather than adding them and I think this way of thinking affects my coding. I think it would be much easier for me to build the desktop version first because then I would have more or less all of my elements. And since my interpretation of mobile is that it is a simplified website where you mostly take things away and rearrange them, it would be easier for me to do mobile second. This isn't based on practicality -- as the article mentions, a lot of websites are more or less mobile only/heavy now. But this is just based on how I think and how I work. In project two I am trying to build my website mobile first, but I am finding it so much harder to do this way. It would definitly be different if it was a mobile only website thus only having one form, but it is not.

Response: When I think about the 7 "whys" exercise when it comes to why am I persuing a career in design I have to admit that I am met with some animosity from myself. My first thought is that I am doing it because it is a way route that allows me to pursue art with a purpose while being applicable to the world. The second why comes with feelings of guilt because I changed from doing a psychology major, which I was so certain that I wanted to do so I didn't explorre any other subjects when started college. The third why is similar to the second why. The third why is because it's what my boyfriend does. My boyfriend is a design major from UO and he has a job in the field. He was the one that introduced me to design in fact and got me thinking about changing to design (although he actually didn't think it would be very fitting for me when I first started). My fourth why is because I don't like being told that I'm not suited to something (even if I'm truly not and in this case I think I actually am suited to some kinds of design work) so I will go out of my way to prove that assessment wrong. My fifth why is because it provides a challenge for me. Not to say that school has been easy for me because it has not, but I find with design I am constantly being challenged and sometimes I rise to the challenge and other times I unfortunately fail. My sixth why is that I have discovered that I actually really enjoy branding and packaging design. And my seventh why is that I can remember all of the times I've skipped a product because the packaging sucked and how many times I have chosen a product purely because of how it looks. And my seventh why is just that I enjoy the feeling of success I get after a succesful project. It is sometimes fleeting, but it makes me feel so good when I finally nail it. I know I didn't completely follow the exercise correctly, but this I felt as though detailed my thought process and feelings better.
